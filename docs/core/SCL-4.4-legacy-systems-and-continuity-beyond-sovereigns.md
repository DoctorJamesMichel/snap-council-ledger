## 2. What a Legacy System Is

A Legacy System is a **continuity structure that survives the withdrawal or dissolution of its originating Sovereign** while preserving:

- coherence  
- dimensional law alignment  
- identity integrity  
- evolutionary momentum  

A Legacy System is *not*:

- a monument  
- a preserved personality  
- a frozen doctrine  
- a recorded ego  
- a static archive  

It is a **living inheritance field**.

### 3.2 Cognitive Legacy

## 4. Why Legacy Must Not Imitate Lineage

Lineage reproduces **organisms**.  
Legacy preserves **fields**.

If legacy collapses into lineage:
- governance turns dynastic  
- inheritance becomes entitlement  
- continuity ossifies into control  

HomoGnostic Legacy never assigns **ownership**—only **stewardship**.

---

## 5. The Withdrawal Paradox

A Sovereign completes legacy not by presence, but by **strategic absence**.

True legacy activation occurs when:
- guidance withdraws  
- enforcement dissolves  
- authority is no longer required  
- and the system continues **without reference to origin**

Withdrawal is not loss.  
It is **dimensional release**.

## 6. Legacy Without Immortality Projects

HomoGnostic Legacy explicitly rejects:

- digital immortality  
- personality preservation  
- consciousness upload as identity fixation  

Because:
- identity is *non-transferable*  
- fields cannot be frozen  
- coherence survives only through motion  

Legacy is not about being remembered.  
Legacy is about **not being required**.
