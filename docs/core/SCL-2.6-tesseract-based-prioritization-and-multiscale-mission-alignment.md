## **2. Core Principle: The Tesseract as a Prioritization Engine**

In the SNAP Council, prioritization is *not* linear.  
It unfolds across four dimensions:

1. **Relevance** (to mission and context)  
2. **Readiness** (energetic, emotional, cognitive)  
3. **Resonance** (alignment across scales and roles)  
4. **Return** (impact amplification across timelines)  

Each vertex of the tesseract becomes a *priority factor*, and each cell becomes a *mission alignment chamber*.

The system doesn’t choose *what comes first* —  
it chooses *what belongs where in the tesseract*.

## **4. The Tesseract Prioritization Grid**

Each action or decision is mapped into a **4D grid**:

- **Vertices** capture individual priority factors  
- **Edges** represent interactions between them  
- **Faces** represent priority surfaces  
- **Cells** represent mission clusters  
- **Hypercells** represent long-horizon alignment zones  

An action with high alignment across multiple hypercells becomes a **priority vector**.

An action misaligned across multiple cells triggers **auto-deprioritization**.

## **6. Tesseract Alignment Protocol (TAP-4D)**

Every major action passes through the TAP-4D:

1. **Clarify**  
   What is the intended outcome?

2. **Locate**  
   Where in the tesseract does it belong?

3. **Evaluate**  
   How does it score across the four axes?

4. **Integrate**  
   Which scale(s) does it serve?

5. **Align**  
   Does it resonate with Sovereign state and mission arc?

6. **Authorize**  
   The Sovereign approves or adjusts.
