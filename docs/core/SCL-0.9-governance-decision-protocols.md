# I. Definition of a Governance Decision

A Governance Decision is a **sovereign, explicit commitment** made by James that:

1. Concludes a deliberation process,  
2. Is not provisional, hypothetical, or exploratory,  
3. Has real-world or Ledger consequences,  
4. Requires accountability and follow-through,  
5. Cannot be attributed to any module or cognitive lens.

A decision is **not**:

- a module output,  
- an insight,  
- a suggestion,  
- a feeling of clarity,  
- or a narrative possibility.

A decision exists **only** when explicitly declared.
