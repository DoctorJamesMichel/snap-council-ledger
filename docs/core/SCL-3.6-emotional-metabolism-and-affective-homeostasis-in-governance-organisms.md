## 2. Emotional Physiology: Signals, Not Stories

In biological organisms, emotions arise from an **intra-organism communication system**:

- hormones and neuromodulators circulate internally  
- these signals shift tissue states and metabolic conditions  
- the brain perceives these shifts as **feelings**  
- meaning is constructed *after* the feeling arises  

The human error is to treat feelings as:

- external truth  
- evidence about situations  
- proof about other people  
- justification for reaction  

In HomoGnostic physiology — and therefore in Governance Organisms — emotions are correctly understood:

> **Emotions = internal signals.**  
> **Feelings = perception of those signals.**  
> **Meaning = cognitive overlay.**

This separation prevents emotional reactivity from contaminating decision geometry.

## 8.1 Misattribution & Emotional Projection Errors

A foundational failure mode in Homo sapiens is emotional misattribution:

- “You made me feel—”  
- “This situation caused my emotions—”  
- “I feel this way because of them—”  

Here, feelings generated by **intra-organism emotional signaling** are projected onto external objects, people, or events.  
This projection fractures emotional responsibility and collapses clarity.

In governance organisms, misattribution takes subtler forms, such as:

- attributing internal emotional states to specific decision pathways  
- confusing atmospheric governance “weather” with external pressure  
- interpreting affective shifts as truth rather than signal  

HomoGnostic governance eliminates this confusion by rooting all emotional signals in **internal physiology** rather than external causation.

> **Emotions arise within.  
> The world does not cause them.  
> It only triggers what is already present.**
