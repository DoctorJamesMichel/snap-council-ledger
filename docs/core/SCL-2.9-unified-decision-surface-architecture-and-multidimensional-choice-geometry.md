## **4. The Four Dimensions of Choice Geometry**

Each decision is evaluated across four geometric dimensions:

### **4.1 Dimensional Relevance**
How relevant is the decision to:

- mission  
- values  
- long-horizon arc  
- Sovereign wellbeing  
- HomoGnostic evolution  

### **4.2 Coherence Gradient**
How does the decision affect:

- personal coherence  
- Council coherence  
- emotional resonance  
- mission unity  

High coherence gradients indicate preferred adjacencies.

### **4.3 Resource Topology**
The “terrain” of resources:

- time  
- energy  
- emotional capacity  
- Council cognition  
- real-world constraints  

Some paths are smoother; others are steep.

### **4.4 Evolutionary Vector Field**
Does the decision contribute to:

- future clarity  
- skill development  
- stewardship expansion  
- HomoGnostic governance capacity  
- dimensional intelligence  

This is the “direction of evolution.”

## **5. Choice Geometry Types**

Within the UDS, every decision falls into one of five geometries:

### **5.1 Convergent Geometry**
Multiple options lead to the same coherent outcome.  
Governance becomes easy and flexible.

### **5.2 Divergent Geometry**
Different paths lead to distinct futures.  
This requires maximizing resonance + future return.

### **5.3 Saddle Geometry**
Some paths are stable in one dimension but unstable in another.  
Requires multi-scale balancing.

### **5.4 Ridge Geometry**
Narrow paths with high reward but also high drift risk.  
Requires strong emotional safety and resource clarity.

### **5.5 Basin Geometry**
Stable, low-drift choices that support restoration or integration.  
Ideal during fatigue or emotional load.

The Council uses this geometry to determine the *correct mode* of decision-making.

## **6. Decision Surface Navigation Protocol (DSNP-4D)**

When a decision arises, the Council uses DSNP-4D:

1. **Map**  
   Identify factors and place them into the UDS.

2. **Shape**  
   Determine geometry: convergent? divergent? ridge?

3. **Sense**  
   Evaluate resonance, coherence, and emotional field.

4. **Evaluate**  
   Apply the four dimensions: relevance, coherence, resource topology, evolutionary vector.

5. **Select**  
   Identify the path with maximal harmonic alignment.

6. **Attune**  
   Slow the cadence to ensure Sovereign clarity.

7. **Proceed**  
   Commit with confidence from multidimensional understanding.

This replaces “I need to choose” with “I need to *see* the geometry.”
