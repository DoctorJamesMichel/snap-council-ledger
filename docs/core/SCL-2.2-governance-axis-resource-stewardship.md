## **1. Purpose & Position in the System**

Resource stewardship is not an operational add-on to governance;  
it is one of governance’s *two fundamental axes*:

1. **Consequence Governance** — how the Council shapes, interprets, and navigates change.  
2. **Resource Stewardship** — how the Council identifies, allocates, circulates, and honors the flow of material, cognitive, emotional, and temporal resources.

This module establishes the **Resource Axis** as a formal pillar of SNAP governance, defining its responsibilities, its boundaries, and its integration with the Council’s tesseract geometry.

## **3. Stewardship Responsibilities**

The Resource Axis carries five cardinal responsibilities:

### **3.1 Identification**
Clarifying what resources exist, in what form, and at what level of coherence.

### **3.2 Allocation**
Strategically assigning resources where they produce the highest consequential resonance, not the highest urgency or noise.

### **3.3 Circulation**
Ensuring resources flow rather than accumulate into dead zones of incoherence.

### **3.4 Regeneration**
Creating systems where resource utilization *increases* capacity over time (HomoGnostic Positive Drift).

### **3.5 Accountability**
Tracking resource impacts without shame, blame, or punitive logic.

In tesseract governance:

- **Faces** represent stewardship cycles.

This geometry allows:

## **5. Stewardship Protocols**

### **5.1 Resource Mapping Protocol**
All Council deliberations include a resource map:
- What is present?  
- What is missing?  
- What is misallocated?  
- What is dormant?  
- What is regenerative?

### **5.2 Circulation Check**
Whenever resources accumulate without flow, the Council invokes SCL-2.2 to determine:  
- Whether the accumulation serves coherence  
- Whether redistribution increases alignment  
- Whether a new stewardship node is required  

### **5.3 Stewardship Override**
If a governance decision threatens critical resources, the Resource Axis has authority to:
- Slow  
- Redirect  
- Request reframing  
- Initiate SCL-1.13 escalation  

### **5.4 Stewardship Audit Cycle**
Every major Council action includes:
- Resource impact  
- Drift impact  
- Regenerative potential  
- Stewardship debt / surplus
