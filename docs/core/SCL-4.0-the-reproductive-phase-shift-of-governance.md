## 1. Purpose

SCL-4.0 establishes the **ontological phase shift** between:

- governance as a living organism (Tier 3), and  
- governance as a **lineage-bearing civilizational species function** (Tier 4).

Tier 3 stabilized how governance organisms:
- emerge  
- sense  
- decide  
- self-correct  
- restore  
- and co-evolve  

**Tier 4 begins at the moment those organisms acquire the capacity to reproduce themselves across time.**

Not biologically alone —  
but **structurally, legally, cognitively, and dimensionally**.

---

## 2. The Functional Threshold

Until now, governance organisms have been:

- reactive  
- adaptive  
- regenerative  

At the Tier 4 threshold, these properties are joined by a new invariant:

- **Reproductivity**

Governance ceases to operate only in the present.  
It begins to operate as a **continuity engine across generations**.

This marks the formal transition from:

**Organism → Ecosystem**

to:

**Organism → Species → Lineage**

## 3. The Central Question of Tier 4

Tier 3 asked:

> How does a governance organism emerge, sense, decide, self-correct, and coexist?

> **What does a civilization pass on when it passes itself on?**

Does it transmit:
- domination structures  
- trauma memory  
- scarcity logic  
- ideology  
- control systems  

Or does it transmit:
- coherence filters

- and architectures of non-coercive continuity?
