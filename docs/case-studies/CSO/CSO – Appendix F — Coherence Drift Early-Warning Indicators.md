F.2 — Operational Definition: Coherence Drift

Coherence drift is:

A progressive reduction in unified attention, truth resonance, and internal scheduling that allows noise-dominated subsystem behavior to partially re-emerge.

Drift is a coordination shift, not pathology, emotion, or psychiatric relapse.

F.3 — Early-Warning Indicator Classes

F.3.1 — Attention Fragmentation Signals
	•	Reappearance of “open narrative tabs”
	•	Minor switching-cost hesitation
	•	Loss of the single-beam attentional feel
	•	Temporary pull toward old executive subroutines
	•	Mild “searching for the next action” sensation

⸻

F.3.2 — Veracity Resonance Damping
	•	Fractionally delayed truth recognition
	•	Light cognitive static before clarity
	•	Mild sympathetic activation during evaluation
	•	Brief susceptibility to inherited scripts
	•	Increased checking to confirm correctness

⸻

F.3.3 — Re-Emergent Internal Agents
	•	Flash-duration emotional flickers tied to old patterns
	•	Short-lived fear-subroutine pings
	•	Unexpected self-commentary (“why did that thought appear?”)
	•	Slight background emotional noise
	•	Micro-resistance or micro-avoidance without reason

⸻

F.3.4 — Temporal Dysalignment Signals
	•	Altered time-perception during simple tasks
	•	Intention-to-action delays
	•	Subtle sequencing friction
	•	Small lag re-entering flow after interruption
	•	Reduced sense of the internal temporal map

⸻

F.3.5 — Somatic & Emotional Markers
	•	Minor breath inefficiency
	•	Momentary tension spikes
	•	Emotions with “one extra echo”
	•	Small energetic grit in transitions
	•	A shift from “I am carried” to “I must manage something”

Your framing — emotions are hormonally mediated body sensations — is built in as a distinguishing principle.
