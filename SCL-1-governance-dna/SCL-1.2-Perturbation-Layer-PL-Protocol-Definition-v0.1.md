# SCL-1.2 — Perturbation Layer (PL)
## Protocol Definition v0.1

**Governance Class:** Epistemic Friction Mechanism  
**Authority:** Operational Governance DNA  
**Revision Status:** v0.1 (Initial Codification)

---

## I. Definition

**Perturbation Layer (PL):**

A structured friction protocol that injects counterfactual, dissenting, or destabilizing hypotheses into deliberation to mitigate correlated blind spot convergence.

PL is not contrarianism.
PL is not adversarial rotation.
PL is not performative dissent.

PL is correlation risk mitigation.

---

## II. Activation Conditions

PL may activate when:

- Convergence appears rapid without dissent artifacts.
- Substrate overlap probability is high.
- Velocity exceeds governance scaling.
- Confidence levels rise disproportionately.
- Minority reasoning is absent or suppressed.

Activation is structural, not personality-driven.

---

## III. Escalation Levels

**Level 1 — Structural Friction Mapping**
- Inject counterfactual hypothesis.
- Map blind spot risk.
- Record activation immutably.

**Level 2 — Directed Counter-Analysis**
- Require structured minority reasoning.
- Evaluate alternative topology.
- Preserve dissent artifact.

**Level 3 — Escalated Stability Audit**
- Freeze advisory output.
- Perform extended cross-case comparison.
- Reassess responsibility topology.

Escalation is recorded immutably.

---

## IV. Logging Requirements

Each PL activation must record:

- Trigger condition.
- Escalation level.
- Core counterfactual hypothesis.
- Confidence delta (pre/post).
- Minority artifact presence.
- Outcome shift (if any).

Logs are append-only.
Logs are immutable.

---

## V. Constraints

PL must:

- Avoid forced adversarial rotation.
- Avoid artificial instability injection.
- Preserve deliberative integrity.
- Escalate proportionally.
- Remain consequence-aligned.

PL exists to preserve epistemic variance.
