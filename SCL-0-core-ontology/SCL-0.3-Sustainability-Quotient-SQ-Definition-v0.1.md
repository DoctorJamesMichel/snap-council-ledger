# SCL-0.3 — Sustainability Quotient (SQ)
## Definition v0.1

**Ontology Class:** Core Evaluative Axis  
**Authority:** Foundational  
**Revision Status:** v0.1 (Initial Codification)

---

## I. Definition

**Sustainability Quotient (SQ):**

A longitudinal measure of structural durability, adaptive capacity, responsibility traceability, dissent preservation, and resilience under consequence over time.

SQ is not a score.
SQ is not a compliance metric.
SQ is not a moral judgment.

SQ is inferred from consequence patterns across time.

Time remains the final arbiter.

---

## II. Primary Axes (v0.1)

SQ is evaluated across the following structural dimensions:

1. **Responsibility Traceability**  
   Clarity of consequence ownership and decision lineage.

2. **Dissent Preservation**  
   Protection of minority reasoning and non-consensus artifacts.

3. **Correlation Resilience**  
   Resistance to monoculture blind spots and substrate convergence.

4. **Velocity–Governance Alignment**  
   Proportional scaling between execution acceleration and oversight mechanisms.

5. **Adaptive Decay Calibration**  
   Capacity to recalibrate sensitivity based on observed consequence patterns.

---

## III. Evaluation Method

SQ is assessed via:

- Immutable historical records.
- Perturbation activation logs.
- Cross-case comparative analysis.
- Fork survival patterns.
- Observed structural durability under stress.

SQ increases when:

- Transparency persists under pressure.
- Responsibility remains traceable.
- Blind spots are corrected without opacity.
- Governance adapts without destabilizing drift.

SQ decreases when:

- Opacity increases.
- Velocity outpaces oversight.
- Dissent artifacts disappear.
- Responsibility topology diffuses.

---

## IV. Constraints

SQ must remain:

- Descriptive, not aspirational.
- Consequence-driven, not predictive.
- Longitudinal, not episodic.
- Structural, not rhetorical.

SNAP does not declare SQ.
SNAP observes and records SQ indicators.
